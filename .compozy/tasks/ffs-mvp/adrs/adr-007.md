# ADR-007: Personal federation in MVP, organizational federation deferred

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-05

## Context

Federation in FFS — the bilateral, contract-based sharing of atoms between sovereign substrates — operates at two scales:

- **Personal federation.** Two individuals (or a small community of peers) share contact graphs and other knowledge selectively. No legal team. No formal contract. Each person authors capability atoms in their own substrate granting the other specific scopes.
- **Organizational federation.** Two firms with legal teams and formal data sharing agreements share matter materials, documents, claims at scale. Bridge contracts are negotiated by counsel, signed by representatives, run via couriers handling thousands of atoms.

Both use the same architectural primitive (signed bilateral contracts with capability-scoped flow). The operational shapes differ.

The success criterion (small community of 5-20 trusted peers using FFS for real) requires personal federation. Organizational federation requires legal review cycles measured in months per partner, which is incompatible with MVP scope.

## Decision

MVP ships personal federation: bilateral capability authoring, peer mounting, tier-based selective sharing, intersection computation, revocation.

Organizational federation is Phase 3, contingent on a counterparty firm being ready and counsel being involved.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Organizational federation in MVP

- **Description:** Build the bridge contract grammar, legal-team-friendly tooling, courier infrastructure for high-volume atom flows, and demonstration deployments with real firms.
- **Pros:** Larger commercial opportunity. Different (and possibly bigger) audience.
- **Cons:** Legal review cycles are slow. Without a counterparty firm ready to sign, this is theoretical work. The success criterion is small-community validation, not enterprise sales.
- **Why rejected:** Out of scope for a personal-hardware MVP with no commercial counterparty.

### Alternative 2: Both in MVP

- **Description:** Ship both personal and organizational federation; the architecture supports both with the same primitive.
- **Pros:** Demonstrates the architectural elegance of one-primitive-two-scales.
- **Cons:** The operational and tooling work for organizational federation is substantial and not tested by the success criterion. Effort that doesn't validate the MVP success criteria is misallocated.
- **Why rejected:** YAGNI. The architectural primitive is the same; demonstrating it at one scale (personal) is sufficient to prove the architecture. The other scale becomes work when its environment exists.

### Alternative 3: Personal federation simplified, no tier-based sharing

- **Description:** Ship personal federation without tier-based selective sharing — just "all atoms about person X are shared or not." Defer tier complexity to Phase 2.
- **Pros:** Simpler MVP. Less classification machinery in the personal-federation path.
- **Cons:** Tier-based selective sharing is the distinctive feature for the contact-graph use case. Without tiers, FFS personal federation is not meaningfully different from a shared address book. The whole point is that you can share existence-only with one peer and full-notes with another.
- **Why rejected:** Tier-based sharing is what makes contact-graph sovereignty real. Cutting it cuts the substrate's distinctive value.

## Consequences

### Positive

- MVP scope is sized to the success criterion. Personal federation is exactly what 5-20 peers in a small community need.
- The same architectural primitive that handles MVP personal federation will handle Phase 3 organizational federation when that environment exists. No re-architecture needed.
- Legal review cycles don't block MVP delivery.

### Negative

- The substrate's enterprise potential is unproven in MVP. Investors, partners, or employers evaluating FFS won't see organizational federation working.
- Phase 3 work for organizational federation depends on Phase 2 work and on a counterparty firm being ready, which is a longer chain.

### Risks

- Personal federation alone may not be enough to prove the substrate's broader value. **Mitigation:** The three motivating scenarios (ADR-004) cover breadth beyond just federation; the substrate's value is shown in contact-graph sovereignty, agent absorption, and clone-and-collaborate together.

## Implementation Notes

The five MVP federation features (capability authoring, peer mounting, tier-based selective sharing, intersection computation, revocation) are individually tractable and collectively validate the architecture.

Multi-peer aggregation (intersection across many peers in one path) is Phase 2 (see ADR-012). MVP federation is bilateral pair-by-pair.

Organizational federation, when it ships in Phase 3, requires additional surfaces: bridge contract authoring with legal review workflow, courier infrastructure for high-volume flows, audit reporting designed for compliance teams, and possibly TEE-hosted deployments for sensitive sectors.
